Bashdar Mahmud Hussen
Assistant Professor
Biography
Dr. Bashdar Mahmud Hussen is an accomplished Iraqi medicinal biotechnologist and Assistant Professor at Lebanese French University in Erbil, Kurdistan, Iraq. Born on January 11, 1981, he holds dual PhDs in Human Genomics (Salahaddin University-Erbil, 2023) and Self-Esteem (Ain Shams University-Cairo, 2014), along with an MSc in Biotechnology from BAMU University, India, and a BSc in Biology from Salahaddin University-Erbil.

With over a decade of research and teaching experience, Dr. Hussen has led national and international research teams, focusing on cancer, non-coding RNAs, and therapeutic targets. He has published approximately more than 300 papers in high-impact journals, earning over 4000 citations and achieving an h-index of 35. His work has been recognized with awards such as “Most Active Researcher of 2021” and ranking as the top researcher in Iraq (2020-2022).

Dr. Hussen has held key academic and administrative roles, including Dean of the College of Health Sciences at Lebanese French University and Director of the Medical Research Centre. Fluent in Kurdish, English, and Arabic, he continues to advance scientific discovery through his prolific research and mentorship.
